Hey There

Welcome to my humble virtual abode.

I am a senior in the School of Foreign Service at Georgetown University, studying Science, Technology, and International Affairs. That's just a fancy way of saying that my thematic academic focus is the nexus of emerging technologies and national security, specializing in (mostly military) unmanned systems and robotics. My technical focus is computer science, which means that I'm the rough equivalent of a CS minor (but less fancy).

I am also interested in commercial drones and robotics, as well as swarms, networks, and systems, among a jillion other things you'll find listed inexhaustively below.

I am graduating in May 2015 (whoa), after which I will be NYC-based. Holla!

If I could accomplish anything regardless of credentials or skills, I would want to be an astronaut (a Martian astronaut!).

Interests

I'm in the midst of writing a thesis on the Global Hawk unmanned aircraft system (UAS), a high-altitude, long endurance drone that conducts intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance (ISR) missions for the US Air Force. The thesis delves into the strategic, technological, competitive, organizational, and political influences that have shaped the evolution of the system, from incubation in DARPA's ACTD program to the present day.
