Hey There



I graduated from the School of Foreign Service at Georgetown University, where I studied Science, Technology, and International Affairs. That's just a fancy way of saying that my thematic academic focus was the nexus of emerging technologies and national security, specializing in (mostly military) unmanned systems and robotics. My technical focus was computer science, coursework that roughly translated to a CS minor.

I am also interested in commercial drones and robotics, as well as swarms, networks, and systems, among many other things you'll find listed inexhaustively below.

I graduated in May 2015, and am now based in NYC.

Interests

I wrote my honors thesis on the Global Hawk unmanned aircraft system (UAS), a high-altitude, long endurance drone that conducts intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance (ISR) missions for the US Air Force. The thesis treats the Global Hawk as only one case of the difficulty of implementing disruptive and transformative technological innovations in a complex and inherently change-averse system. it delves into the strategic, technological, competitive, organizational, and political influences that have shaped the evolution of the system, from incubation in DARPA's ACTD program to the present day.
